What is Hinachi Ai?

Hinachi Ai is an advanced AI Illustrator created by Xeno Gen Projects that primarily target for “Toon-Anime” art style. Even tho, its primarily build for “Toon-Anime” art style. It’s not limited, Hinachi Ai can also produce realistic art style.

Hinachi Ai was public available in November 3, 2022 but on October 14, 2023 it become a private tool for Xeno Gen Projects due to inefficient budget to maintain for public use.

How to get best artwork from Hinachi Ai?

  • Let Hinachi Ai to decide to whether its full body or upper body.

¬†

  • Make your description very simple that Hinachi Ai can interpret. Complex and too many details can make Hinachi failed to interpret and give you less quality even with the help of the renderer.

¬†

  • Avoid using “standing”, “sitting”, “etc…”. because it will give you exactly that. Make your character alive and make Hinachi Ai do it for you or use Image Allusion instead.

¬†

  • Avoid something that make character hold on too. Example: She is holding a mic, she is holding glass of water, etc… (Sometimes, AI Correction failed to correct them)

¬†

  • Avoid using “Town”, “Office”, “City”, “etc…” if you want good background instead use “Clean town”, “Busy with office work”, “Wonderful city” but if you want simple background avoid using “Simple Town”, “Simple Office”, “Simple City” instead use “Town”, “Office”, “City”.

¬†

  • Each renderer is unique but it’s important to choose the correct renderer for your artwork. If you are aiming for Illustration, choose the ‘Exemplar Renderer’ and when you do not aim for Illustration, but you aim for details choose the ‘Exhaustive Renderer’

Do Hinachi Ai ignore some certain words?

Do not add something like “Illustration”, “Best Quality”, “etc…”. Hinachi Ai doesn’t read that, but it will only read the important details such as “Girl in a park” rather than “Best Quality of a girl in a park” or “Illustration of a girl in a park”. Then the renderer will come in play to improve the detail of that artwork made by Hinachi Ai.
